Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about fitness, nutrition, and using our platform. If you can't find what you're looking for, feel free to reach out through our contact page.

Getting Started

How do I get personalized advice from your specialists?

You can reach our specialists through the contact form on our Contact page. Select your area of interest (fitness, nutrition, or mobility), and the appropriate specialist will respond within 48 hours.

Do I need to create an account to read the blog posts?

No, all our blog posts are freely available to read. Creating an account allows you to interact with our community and receive updates about future features.

Fitness Training

How often should I work out as a beginner?

For beginners, we recommend starting with 2-3 workouts per week, allowing your body time to recover between sessions. Focus on learning proper form and gradually increase frequency as your fitness improves.

What's the best way to prevent workout injuries?

Injury prevention starts with proper warm-up, correct form, and gradual progression. Always start with lighter weights to master the movement patterns, and don't hesitate to ask our specialists for form checks.

Nutrition & Diet

How do I calculate my daily caloric needs?

While individual needs vary, you can estimate your base caloric needs using your weight, height, age, and activity level. Our nutrition specialist can help you determine a more precise target for your specific goals.

Should I track my macronutrients?

Tracking macronutrients can be helpful for understanding your eating patterns and optimizing your nutrition. However, start with building healthy eating habits and food awareness before diving into detailed tracking.

Mobility & Movement

How often should I do mobility work?

Incorporate basic mobility work daily, even if it's just 5-10 minutes. Focus on areas that feel tight or restricted, and consider longer mobility sessions 2-3 times per week.

Can I improve flexibility at any age?

Yes, flexibility can be improved at any age through consistent, appropriate stretching and mobility work. Progress may be gradual, but regular practice will lead to noticeable improvements.
